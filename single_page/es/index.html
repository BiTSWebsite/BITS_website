about BITS

objective

BITS stimulate a global debate on the changing meaning of technological sovereignty and explore the ways in which various forms of sovereignty - of citizenship, cities, states and regions - can subsist within global conditions today . BITS will focus on the political effects of technological change and explore how the growth of technology platforms and data extraction that these possible exercise transformations on governments, work organization, ownership and access to basic needs such as water, food, housing and energy. This task is especially important given the current socio-economic reformulation around the theoretical and practical core of digital technology with a powerful new alliance between the military-industrial complex technology companies and global finance.

Activities

BITS initiatives include a symposium to be held on 8 and 9 December 2017 in Barcelona. Also a series of monthly lectures and workshops linking researchers, citizens, social movements and officials, which will begin in 2017. On the website of BITS will be published and share content regularly, including briefings research and digests news related issues addressed by the initiative.

Seminars and lines of analysis and research will aim to answer questions such as 'what do you do?'. Among its participants are prominent academics, journalists, researchers, activists, social movements, campaigns developers, entrepreneurs and public officials. Thus, in addition to promoting awareness and stimulate common theoretically robust debates, BITS attracts and promotes concrete examples and suggestions for specific policies and interventions in the urban context.

Events

technological sovereignty: a new challenge for cities?

Welcome to the presentation of Barcelona Initiative for Technological Sovereignty (BITS), an interdisciplinary joint effort that will analyze and will shape ingrained urban technology program on democracy, solidarity and autonomy.

BITS originates from the interest in developing a strategic vision of using technology for the benefit of an alternative, social and economic policy agenda. Based in the city of Barcelona, ​​BITS brings together intellectuals, academics and a network of activists from different social movements with extensive experience in the field of technology and geopolitics. It also has the Internet Interdisciplinary Institute (IN3 / UOC) and the Institute of Government and Public Policy (IGOP / UAB).
