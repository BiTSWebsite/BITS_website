About BITS

Purpose

BITS will stimulate a global debate about the changing meanings of sovereignty and explore the ways in which various types of sovereignty – of citizens, cities, nation states, and regions – can still be maintained in today’s highly technological global conditions. With a strong focus on the political effects of technological change, BITS will explore how the rise of technology platforms and the data extractivism they enable is transforming governments, labor, ownership, and access to the basics of life such as water, food, housing, and energy. This task is particularly important as the current political and economic regime reformulates itself around the rhetorical and practical kernel of digital technology, with a new mighty alliance between technology firms, global finance, and the military-industrial complex.

Activities

BITS’ initiatives include a Symposium on 8-9 December 2017 in Barcelona, and a series of monthly lectures and workshops linking researchers, grassroots, and public officials starting in 2017. Regular content will be produced and shared, including research briefings and news digests pertaining to the questions addressed by the initiative.

BITS seminars, work streams, and research lines are geared towards ‘what’s to be done?’ sort of questions. Its members and audience include prominent academics, journalists, researchers, social movement activists, campaigners, entrepreneurs, and public officials from municipal and national governments in attendance. Thus, while BITS raises awareness and stimulates robust theoretical discussions, it also brings in and encourages concrete examples and suggestions for specific policy interventions in the urban context.

Who

The Barcelona Initiative for Technological Sovereignty (BITS) is very excited to announce its October 7 inaugural conference: ‘Technological Sovereignty: A New Challenge for Cities?’

This free and open event will feature keynotes by Paul Mason, journalist and author of PostCapitalism: A Guide to our Future; Evgeny Morozov, author of To Save Everything, The Folly of Technological Solutionism, and Anna Minton, author of Ground Control: Fear and Happiness in the Twenty-First Century City. Have a look at the event agenda.

Among its participants are prominent academics, journalists, researchers, activists from social movements, developers, campaigners, entrepreneurs and public servants. Based in the city of Barcelona, BITS is a strategic alliance between the Internet Interdisciplinary Institute of the Open University of Catalonia (IN3/UOC), the Hans Crescent Symposium, the Institute of Government and Public Policies (IGOP) of the Autonomous University of Barcelona (UAB), and the Centre de Cultura Contemporània de Barcelona (CCCB).

Events

Technological Sovereignty: A New Challenge for Cities?

Welcome to the launch of the Barcelona Initiative for Technological Sovereignty (BITS), a multidisciplinary effort to analyze and shape an urban technological agenda rooted in the ideals of democracy, solidarity, and autonomy.

BITS originates from the partners’ shared interest in developing a strategic vision of using advanced information technologies to benefit an alternative political, social, and economic agenda. Hosted by the City of Barcelona and bringing together leading intellectuals, academics and a network of activists from different social movements with wide range of experience in the fields of technology and geopolitics. In addition, BITS collaborates with the Internet Interdisciplinary Institute (IN3/UOC) and the Institute of Government and Public Policy (IGOP/UAB).
